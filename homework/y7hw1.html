Part A: Variables in Experiments

Key Definitions

Independent Variable: The factor that YOU CHANGE in an experiment (the thing you're testing).

Dependent Variable: The factor that you MEASURE to see the results (what happens because of your change).

Control Variables: The factors you KEEP THE SAME to make the test fair.

Variables - Example

Salt Water vs Pure Water Heating

Remember when we heated salt water and pure water to see which heated faster?

  • Independent Variable: Type of liquid - we changed whether we used salt water or pure water
  • Dependent Variable: Temperature - we measured how hot the liquid got after 5 minutes
  • Control Variables:
    • Volume of liquid (50ml) - If one beaker had more water, it would take longer to heat (unfair test!)
    • Size of Bunsen flame - If one flame was bigger, that liquid would heat faster (unfair test!)

Part C: Precision in Measurements

What is Precision?

Precision: How small a change you can measure. Equipment with smaller divisions is MORE PRECISE.

The smaller the divisions on your measuring equipment, the more precise your measurement!

Precision - Examples

Example: Measuring Water

Beaker: Has divisions every 10 ml (10, 20, 30...)

Measuring cylinder: Has divisions every 1 ml (1, 2, 3...)

‚Üí The measuring cylinder is MORE PRECISE because it has smaller divisions!

Ancient Candle Clocks

Long ago, people used candles to measure time!

Candle A: Has marks every hour

Candle B: Has marks every 15 minutes

‚Üí Candle B is MORE PRECISE for measuring time!
